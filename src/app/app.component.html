About

I have started my journey as a software engineer in 2009, shortly before graduating from university. I spent 2 years working as a student partner at Microsoft, teaching programming in C#/.net to my fellow students and doing some smaller projects in various Microsoft related technologies. The first 3 years of my career I spent mostly as a backend developer and a client-side Windows developer.

In 2014, I decided to focus more on the Web where AngularJS (and later Angular) emerged as my framework of choice. I have been working with Angular ever since and after 6 years and around 7 projects I have gained significant proficiency in the framework.

In the past 4 years I often played the role of a mentor to junior developers or student workers, teaching them software architecture, developing skill trees, exercises, establishing communities of practice etc. It was something I very much enjoyed.

I was also fortunate to take part in developing several digital products from the ground up. I gained knowledge about product management, agile development/scrum and UX. I enjoy working in cross-functional teams and my ideal employer would have an open platform for discussing ideas in contrast to hard hierarchies.
